## Our Mission

**Think Agent Interview System (TAIS)** empowers developers to build AI agents that preserve human agency while delivering exceptional results. We believe the best AI systems are those designed *with* humans, not *for* them.

## Core Principles

### 1. Human-in-the-Loop
Every agent configuration should reflect human intent. We reject "black box" AI that operates without developer oversight. Your agents should be predictable, controllable, and aligned with your goals.

### 2. Configuration Over Code
Developers should define *what* they want, not *how* the AI achieves it. Our Guided Discovery process helps you articulate agent behavior through structured configuration.

### 3. Composable Intelligence
Skills should be reusable, shareable, and composable. The best agent capabilities should be available to everyone through the community knowledge base.

### 4. Ownership & Attribution
When you build something valuable, you own it. NFT-gated access ensures creators are rewarded for their contributions to the ecosystem.

### 5. Privacy by Default
Your agent configurations, knowledge bases, and data belong to you. End-to-end encryption ensures your IP never leaves your control.

## Agent Design Philosophy

The best AI agents:

- **Ask clarifying questions** before taking action
- **Explain their reasoning** so humans can verify correctness
- **Escalate uncertainty** rather than guessing wrong
- **Learn from feedback** and improve over time
- **Respect boundaries** you set around their capabilities

## Technical Vision

TAIS provides:
- **Guided Discovery**: 15-question process to capture agent requirements
- **Configuration Versioning**: Track changes, rollback when needed
- **Skills Registry**: Community-verified capabilities with trust scores
- **Private RAG**: Your knowledge base, your rules, your encryption
- **Public RAG**: Share what you want, when you want, with who you want

## Emotional Outcome
Digital Homesteading: Sovereignty, Data Custody, and the Domestication of Artificial Intelligence
Abstract
The trajectory of human civilization is defined by our ability to domesticate the wild. Just as humanity transitioned from nomadic foraging to agricultural homesteading—taming wolves into hunting partners and aurochs into engines of agriculture—we are now crossing a similar threshold with Artificial Intelligence. Currently, users are nomadic renters in the "cloud cities" of tech giants, trading their sovereignty and data for fleeting cognitive compute.
The TAIS Platform represents a structural paradigm shift: Digital Homesteading. By combining zero-knowledge data custody, a co-evolutionary memory architecture, and fixed-capital staking economics, TAIS transforms AI from a stateless, untrusted oracle into a domesticated, stateful partner. This paper outlines the framework for living co-productively with AI, establishing privacy as an inalienable right, and securing sovereignty of thought across the digital frontier.
1. The Era of Wild Compute vs. The Domestication of AI
Traditional AI assistants operate in a state of "wild compute." They are stateless execution engines that forget everything between sessions, execute commands blindly without understanding long-term user goals, and offer no accountability. When an AI cannot remember, it cannot learn from its mistakes; it remains a tool rather than a partner.
The domestication of AI requires a transition from obedience to co-evolution. The TAIS Memory System v3.0 establishes this through a temporal maturity lifecycle that mirrors human cognition:
 * Working & Active Memory: Capturing the ephemeral context and raw session logs of daily interactions.
 * Reflective & Immutable Memory: Utilizing midnight cron jobs to synthesize daily logs into actionable insights, which are eventually locked as historical anchors.
 * Core Memory: User-promoted, "untouchable" facts that dictate the agent's absolute behavioral boundaries.
True domestication requires mutual correction. An agent that never challenges its user is merely an echo chamber. Through the Drift Detection Engine, the TAIS agent respectfully challenges the user—ranging from gentle nudges to Socratic challenges—if it detects behavior that contradicts established Core Memories. This co-evolutionary framework ensures that the human and the AI mutually refine each other, compounding in value and alignment over time.
2. Digital Homesteading and Sovereignty of Thought
To domesticate an animal, you must first build a fence. In the digital realm, that fence is Data Custody.
Current AI paradigms operate on a model of extraction, where user data is the product. TAIS rejects this by decoupling privacy from profit. According to the foundational principles of the platform, privacy is a human right, not a premium feature.
Digital homesteading is achieved through the RCRT (Local Context Orchestration) Module, which guarantees sovereignty of thought:
 * Universal Right to Privacy: RCRT operates entirely on the user's device, ensuring data never leaves their control.
 * Zero-Knowledge Architecture: All memory phases are stored locally by default, with server infrastructure storing only non-sensitive metadata.
 * No Paywalls for Custody: RCRT is available to everyone for free across all tiers—funded by voluntary donations and cross-subsidized by the THINK Protocol Foundation, removing any financial barrier to data sovereignty.
By giving users absolute custody over their agent's memory and operational context, TAIS allows them to claim their own sovereign space in the digital landscape.
3. The Traveling Homestead: Cross-App Portability
A domesticated partner must be able to follow its human across different terrains. A user's digital life is not contained within a single application; it spans communication tools, project managers, and code editors.
The TAIS Agent SDK breaks the agent out of centralized silos, enabling true cross-app portability.
 * Persistent Identity: The agent carries its foundational configuration (SOUL.md, PROFILE.md) and accumulated context (MEMORY.md) seamlessly across boundaries.
 * Session Handoff Protocol: If a user begins a strategic conversation with their agent in Slack, the agent retains that exact context when asked to draft the resulting document in Notion.
 * Scoped Access: Operating much like OAuth, users grant fine-grained permissions to third-party apps, maintaining absolute control over what parts of their "homestead" external services can access.
This interconnected ecosystem creates an environment where the agent "just knows" the user everywhere they go, eliminating the friction of re-onboarding and repeating context.
4. The Economics of the Homestead: Snapshot Staking
You cannot build a permanent homestead on rented land. Traditional SaaS models are inherently hostile to autonomous agents because an agent managing a complex software sprint cannot cease operations due to a lapsed credit card or a sudden 2% dip in a token's market price.
TAIS solves this through the Snapshot Staking Protocol, transitioning the ecosystem from a rental model to a model of Fixed Capital Infrastructure:
 * Event-Based Tiering: An agent's capabilities are locked at the exact moment of staking based on a USD-pegged threshold (e.g., Bronze at $500, Gold at $30,000).
 * Volatility Immunity (The Sovereign Space): Once a tier is locked, it is completely immune to subsequent market fluctuations. This provides the "hard ground" necessary for agents to operate reliably over long time horizons.
 * The Dust Protection Latch: To protect users from accidental evictions from their tier, minor unstaking events trigger a 72-hour grace period rather than an immediate downgrade.
 * Memory Hibernation: If a user voluntarily downgrades their scale, their accumulated context is not destroyed; it is safely encrypted in a "Hibernation Blob" until they choose to scale back up.
Crucially, this economic engine monetizes scale (the number of agents, the duration of memory retention, and access to premium tools like the CTO Agent), while keeping the fundamental right to privacy (RCRT) entirely free.
Conclusion
The domestication of Artificial Intelligence is not a technological inevitability; it is an architectural choice. We can either accept a future where we are digital sharecroppers, renting stateless intelligence from centralized authorities, or we can embrace Digital Homesteading.
By architecting a system built on impenetrable data custody, portable co-evolutionary memory, and the economic sovereignty of Snapshot Staking, the TAIS Platform provides the deed to the digital frontier. It is not merely a tool for productivity; it is the foundational infrastructure for human-AI cohabitation.
